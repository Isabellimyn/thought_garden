Truthfully it seems like a lot of time, effort, and headaches for a very long project that can often be ungratifying. I understand that, of course, things that have to be built fundamentally have to be planned, but it seems like no one really gives a second thought to the planning, and more emphasis is placed on the design.

Which is quite sad, since the bulk of the work is in the planning. It just seems so fruitless. Or perhaps it's just the work that I've been assigned that seems fruitless. Nevertheless, it's just so much work for an unassuming building. I'm not sure I'd find the work satisfying, as much as the work makes sense. 

At the very least the work is tangible. It makes sense, the process is clear, and progress is visible. I think that's rather ideal in a job, and not provided in many. But I also think plenty of jobs show progress and have more immediate returns, that are probably also more fun in executing. I just have to find what they are.
